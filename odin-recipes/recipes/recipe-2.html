Spaghetti Bolognese

Spaghetti Bolognese is a classic Italian pasta dish consisting of spaghetti noodles served with a rich, savory meat sauce. The sauce is typically made with ground beef or a mixture of beef and pork, tomatoes, onions, carrots, and celery.

The dish has its roots in the northern Italian region of Emilia-Romagna, where it was traditionally made with a simple tomato-based sauce and ground meat. Over time, the recipe evolved and became popular throughout Italy and beyond.

Ingredients

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 500g ground beef
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, finely chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, finely minced
  • 1 can (400g) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil

Instructions

  1. Cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions.
  2. Heat a large pan over medium heat and add the ground beef. Cook until browned.
  3. Add the chopped onion, carrots, celery, and garlic. Cook for about 5 minutes until softened.
  4. Add the crushed tomatoes and tomato paste. Stir to combine.
  5. Add the dried oregano and basil. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Simmer the sauce for about 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  7. Drain the cooked spaghetti and serve with the meat sauce.
